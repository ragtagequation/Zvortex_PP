We collect both personal and non-personal information to provide and improve our services. The types of information we collect include:

  • Communication Data: If you contact us for support or inquiries, we may collect details of your communication, including your name, email, and the content of your message.

1.2 Non-Personal Information

  • Technical Information: We may collect information about your device, browser type, operating system, IP address, and device identifiers.
  • Usage Data: We collect information about your interactions with our website and services, including access times, page views, and features used.
  • Tracking Information: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to gather information about your browsing habits and website activity.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for a variety of purposes, including to:

  • Provide Services: Use your data to create, manage, and optimize ad campaigns, including using third-party APIs to deploy ads across different platforms.
  • Process Payments: Use your payment information to process and fulfill transactions for our services.
  • Improve User Experience: Analyze usage patterns to improve the functionality of our website and services.
  • Customer Support: Respond to your inquiries, provide troubleshooting, and improve customer satisfaction.
  • Marketing and Communication: Send you promotional materials, updates about new features, or customer satisfaction surveys. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time.

We do not sell or rent your personal information. However, we may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • In Legal Proceedings: We may disclose your information if required by law, court order, or governmental regulation, or if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.
  • In Business Transfers: If Zvortex is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies (such as web beacons and pixels) to collect information about your browsing activity on our website, including:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that remain on your device during your session and are deleted when you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a set period and help us recognize you when you return to our website.
  • Third-Party Cookies: These are placed by third-party services, such as analytics or ad platforms, to collect usage data and track the effectiveness of ads.

You can adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies, but doing so may limit your access to certain features of our services.

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy or as required by law. This includes retaining your account information and business data for the duration of your use of the service and for a reasonable period after your account is closed.

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable physical, technical, and administrative security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. This includes:

  • Encryption of sensitive data, such as payment information, during transmission using secure socket layer (SSL) technology.
  • Access controls to limit the number of individuals who can access personal data.

While we strive to protect your information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is entirely secure. Therefore, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your data to such countries. We will take all reasonable measures to ensure your data is protected in accordance with applicable laws.
